The Gervais Principle P2

熱爾韋原理 2

The Gervais Principle II: Posturetalk, Powertalk, Babytalk and Gametalk

November 11, 2009 By Venkatesh Rao

原文網址:
https://www.ribbonfarm.com/2009/11/11/the-gervais-principle-ii-posturetalk-powertalk-babytalk-and-gametalk/

We began this analysis of corporate life by exploring a theoretical construct (the Gervais Principle) through the character arcs of Michael and Ryan in The Office. The construct and examples provide a broad-strokes treatment of the why of the power dynamics among Sociopaths, the Clueless and Losers.

我們通過《辦公室》中邁克爾和瑞安的人物弧線，探索了一種理論構架（熱爾韋原理），從而開始了對企業生活的分析。這一理論構架和實例概括性地闡述了反社會者、無知者和失敗者之間權力動態的原因。

This helps us understand how the world works, but not how to work it. So let me introduce you to the main skill required here, mastery over the four major languages spoken in organizations, among Sociopaths, Losers and the Clueless. I’ll call the four languages Posturetalk, Powertalk, Babytalk and Gametalk. Here’s a picture of who speaks what to whom. Let’s use it to figure out how to make friends and influence people, Office style.

這理論有助於我們理解這個世界是如何運作的，但卻還不知道如何去運用。因此，讓我向你介紹這裏所需的主要技能，即掌握反社會者、失敗者和無知者在組織中所使用的四種主要語言。我把這四種語言稱為 “姿態語”（Posturetalk）、”權力語”（Powertalk）、”寶寶語”（Babytalk）和 “遊戲語”（Gametalk）。下面是誰對誰說什麽的圖片。讓我們用它來了解如何以辦公室風格交友和影響他人。

大意：懂熱爾韋原理還不夠。組織中有四種語言，圖中顯示誰對誰說哪一種。

The Calculus of Organizational Dynamics 組織動態計算

The Gervais Principle operates at the slow tempo of promotions, demotions, layoffs and hirings. The bulk of organizational life, however, plays out much faster. One conversation at a time. The different species in the organization speak different languages. If the MacLeod Hierarchy and Lifecycle provide the space and time, and the Gervais Principle is Newton’s law, the various languages together constitute calculus. You have to learn calculus before you can do anything useful with the theory.

熱爾韋原理的運作節奏緩慢，包括晉升、降職、裁員和招聘。然而，組織生活的大部分時間卻要快得多。一次一次對話。組織中不同的人說著不同的語言。如果說麥克勞德層次理論和生命周期理論提供了空間和時間，熱爾維原理是牛頓定律，那麽各種語言就共同構成了微積分。你必須先學會微積分，才能用理論做任何有用的事情。

大意：熱爾韋原理只說明了晉升、降職、裁員和招聘。學會四種語言的用法，才能在日常對話中應用。

Among our three groups — Sociopaths, Clueless and Losers, we have four unique languages. Powertalk is the in-group language of the Sociopaths, and that’s what we’ll talk about in this post. Posturetalk is the language spoken by the clueless to everybody (they don’t have an in-group language since they don’t realize they constitute a group). Sociopaths and Losers talk back to the Clueless in a language called Babytalk that seems like Posturetalk to the Clueless. I’ll cover Posturetalk and Babytalk in the next installment. Among themselves, Losers speak a language called Gametalk. This is the only language that has been properly studied and documented. I won’t cover it at all, but you can learn all about it in the pop classics on Transactional Analysis (TA, a Neo-Freudian school) from 30 years ago (now available in updated editions): Eric Berne’s Games People Play and What Do You Say after You Say Hello and Thomas Harris’ I’m OK–You’re OK. Yes they’re dated and have been parodied to the point that they seem campy today. No that does not mean they are useless. Yes, you need a brain to read them critically today. Add these three books to the two I already referenced (The Organization Man and Images of Organization).

在我們的三個群體–反社會者、無知者和失敗者中，我們有四種獨特的語言。 “權力語 “是反社會者的組內語言，我們將在這篇文章中討論它。 “姿態語 “是無知者對所有人說的語言（他們沒有組內語言，因為他們沒有意識到自己是一個群體）。反社會者和失敗者用一種叫做 “寶寶語 “的語言回敬無知者，這種語言在無知者看來就像是 “姿態語 “。我將在下一篇文章中介紹姿態語和寶寶語。失敗者之間使用一種叫做 “遊戲語 “的語言。這是唯一一種經過適當研究和記錄的語言。我就不一一介紹了，但你可以在 30 年前的《情感分析》（TA，新弗洛伊德學派）流行經典中了解到它的全部內容（現在有了更新版本）： 埃裏克-伯恩（Eric Berne）的《人們玩的遊戲》（Games People Play）和《打招呼後你會說什麽》（What Do You Say after You Say Hello），以及托馬斯-哈裏斯（Thomas Harris）的《我沒事，你也沒事》（I’m OK-You’re OK）。是的，它們已經過時了，而且被戲仿得很厲害，以至於在今天看來很乏味。但這並不意味著它們一無是處。是的，今天要批判性地閱讀它們，你需要一個頭腦。除了我已經提到的兩本書（《組織人》和《組織的形象》）之外，再加上這三本。

大意：四種語言使用的人跟對象。失敗者之間說的是遊戲語我就不說了，自己去看那三本書。

Finally, Sociopaths and Losers speak rarely to each other at all. One of the functions of the Clueless, recall, is to provide a buffer in what would otherwise be a painfully raw master-slave dynamic in a pure Sociopath-Loser organization. But when they do talk, they actually speak an unadorned language you could call Straight Talk if it were worth naming. It is the ordinary (if rare) utilitarian language of the sane, with no ulterior motives flying around. The mean-what-you-say-and-say-what-you-mean stuff between two people in a fixed, asymmetric power relationship, who don’t want or need to play real or fake power games. This is the unmarked black triangle edge in the diagram.

最後，反社會者和失敗者之間很少交談。要知道，”無知者 “的作用之一，就是在一個純粹的反社會者–失敗者組織中，提供一個緩衝，否則，主從之間的關係就會變得非常生硬。但是，當他們說話時，他們實際上說的是一種不加修飾的語言，如果值得命名的話，你可以稱之為 “直言不諱 “（Straight Talk）。這是理智者的普通（如果罕見的話）功利語言，沒有任何不可告人的目的。這是在固定的、不對稱的權力關系中的兩個人之間的 “怎麼想就怎麼說/心口如一 “，他們不想也不需要玩真實或虛假的權力遊戲。這就是圖中沒有標記的黑色三角邊。

大意：反社會主管跟失敗者員工之間的黑線就是有話直說，很少發生，因為主僕角色太明顯會讓人不舒服。

Let’s do the most important language, Powertalk.

讓我們來學習最重要的語言–權力語。

The Elements of Powertalk 權力語的要素

Here are two examples, of good and bad Powertalk respectively.

這裏有兩個例子，分別說明了好的和壞的權力語。

Fluent Powertalk

流利的權力語

At a Dunder-Mifflin management party, shortly after Michael and Jan disclose their affair to David Wallace, per HR requirements, Wallace casually invites Jim to blow off the party for a while and shoot hoops in the backyard. Once outside, Wallace nonchalantly asks, “So what’s up with Jan and Michael?” He is clearly fishing for information, having observed the bizarre couple dynamics at the party.

在一次 Dunder-Mifflin 公司的管理層聚會上，就在邁克爾和簡按照人力資源部的要求向大衛-華萊士公開他們的戀情後不久，華萊士隨口邀請吉姆暫時離開聚會，到後院投籃。一到外面，華萊士就不緊不慢地問：”那簡和邁克爾是怎麽回事？” 他顯然是在打探消息，因為他觀察到了派對上那對奇怪的情侶的動態。

Jim replies, “I wouldn’t know…*(pregnant pause)…*where to begin.” (slight laugh)

吉姆回答說：”我不知道……（意味深長的停頓）……從何說起”。(微微一笑）

David Wallace laughs in return. This is as eloquent as such a short fragment of Powertalk can get. Here are just some of the messages being communicated by the six words and the meaningful pause and laugh.

大衛-華萊士也笑了。這樣一個簡短的權力語片段就已經很有說服力了。以下是這六個字以及意味深長的停頓和笑聲所傳達的一些信息。

  • Message 1: It is a complex situation (literal).
    信息 1：情況很復雜（字面意思）。

  • Message 2: I understand you think something bizarre is going on. I am confirming your suspicion. It is a bizarre mess, and you should be concerned.
    信息 2：我知道你認為發生了一些奇怪的事情。我正在證實你的猜測。這真是一團糟，你應該擔心。

  • Message 3: This is the first significant conversation between us, and I am signaling to you that I am fluent in Powertalk.
    信息 3：這是我們之間的第一次重要對話，我在向你表示我精通權力語。

  • Message 4: I know how to communicate useful information while maintaining plausible deniability.
    信息 4：我知道如何在傳達有用信息的同時保持似是而非的可信度。

  • Message 5: I am not so gratified at this sign of attention from you that I am going to say foolish things that could backfire on me.
    信息 5：我不會因為你對我的關注而欣喜若狂，以至於說一些會適得其反的蠢話。

  • Message 6: I am aware of my situational leverage and the fact that you need me. I am not so overawed that I am giving it all up for free.
    信息 6：我意識到自己的優勢和你需要我的事實。我並沒有被嚇倒，以至於白白放棄這一切。

  • Message 7: I am being non-committal enough that you can pull back or steer this conversation to safer matters if you like. I know how to give others wiggle room, safe outs and exits.
    信息 7： 我不做任何承諾，如果你願意，你可以把話題拉回來，或者引導到更安全的事情上。我知道如何給別人回旋的餘地、安全的出口和出口。

  • Message 8: You still have to earn my trust. But let’s keep talking. What do you have that I could use?
    信息 8： 你仍然需要贏得我的信任。不過，我們還是繼續聊吧。你有什麽我可以利用的東西？

The key here is that only Message 1 is comprehensible to the truly Clueless; this is what makes for plausible deniability. You cannot prove that the other messages were exchanged. Losers can partially understand, but not speak Powertalk. To them, Powertalk is a spectator sport.

這裏的關鍵在於，只有信息 1 才能被真正的 “無知者 “理解；這也是合理推諉的原因。你無法證明其他信息也被交換過。失敗者能部分理解，但不會說權力語。對他們來說，權力語只是一項觀賞運動。

大意：權力語有字面能理解的意思，無知者只能看懂這層。其他的含意經過編碼，失敗者或許能讀懂，但是自己不能掌握說的技巧。

We can speculate with a fair amount of certainty what someone like Michael would have said in such a situation if his and Jim’s roles had been reversed. He would have been so gratified by the attention that he would have babbled out an incoherent and epic narrative without further prompting. Wallace would have taken the information and walked away without paying.

我們可以相當肯定地推測，如果邁克爾和吉姆的角色顛倒過來，他在這種情況下會說些什麽。他一定會因為受到關注而欣喜若狂，以至於在沒有進一步提示的情況下滔滔不絕地講述自己的故事。華萊士則會拿了信息，不付錢就走人。

大意：無知者感知不到暗藏的信息，就會白白被利用。

A Powertalk Trainwreck

撞車的權力語

Here is the second example, illustrating Michael’s inability to speak Powertalk. This is during Michael’s salary negotiation with Jan, again shortly after their affair has been revealed and there is a clear conflict of interest to maneuver around. Much to Michael’s dismay, Jan insists on Toby’s presence, to maintain a witnessed appearance of perfect due process.

下面是第二個例子，說明邁克爾不會說權力語。這是在邁克爾與簡進行薪酬談判時，同樣是在他們的戀情曝光後不久，有明顯的利益衝突需要周旋。令邁克爾大失所望的是，簡堅持要托比在場，以維持一個完美正當程序的有目共睹的表象。

Jan offers Michael a modest raise, which he knows (thanks to being coached by Darryl, as we saw in the last post) to be a lowball offer. He is shocked. He feels betrayed. He has no idea it might be useful to hide his inner reaction with Toby present. His response:

簡給邁克爾提出了一個微薄的加薪，他知道（多虧了達裏爾的指導，我們在上一篇文章中已經看到）這是一個低價。他感到震驚。他覺得自己被背叛了。他不知道在托比在場的情況下，隱藏自己內心的反應可能會有幫助。他的回答是

“Jan… After all we’ve been through…” (with a hurt, puppy-dog look in his eyes)

“簡……在我們經歷了這麽多之後……”（眼神中流露出受傷的小狗般的神情）

Jan struggles desperately to return to the necessary script of due process. Toby, in one of his rare (and revealing) displays of perfect Schadenfreude, starts scribbling furiously and gleefully. A dull and routine HR role has suddenly turned meaty. A train wreck is imminent. When Michael furiously asks him what he is scribbling, Toby mutters under his breath, “taking notes for the deposition.”

簡拼命掙扎，試圖回到必要的正當程序劇本中。托比罕見地（露骨地）表現出完美的幸災樂禍，開始興高采烈地塗鴉筆記。枯燥乏味的人力資源工作突然變得有滋有味起來。一場火車失事即將發生。當邁克爾怒氣沖沖地問他在寫什麽時，托比小聲嘟囔道：”為取證做筆記。”

A cut later, we see that Jan has given up trying to get Michael to talk on the two seamless levels that a Powertalk script would have required. She switches to Babytalk, hopelessly attempting to separate an official on-the-record talk track with a through-gritted-teeth coaching track. Finally, she gives up and openly succumbs to the conflict of interest by revealing her negotiating position completely.

一個剪輯之後，我們看到簡已經放棄了讓邁克爾在兩個無縫層面上說話的努力，而這正是權力語的劇本所要求的。她轉而使用寶寶語，無望地試圖將正式的記錄談話軌跡與咬牙切齒的指導軌跡區分開來。最後，她放棄了，公開屈服於利益衝突，完全暴露了自己的談判立場。

She says, “Michael, I can give you 12%, but you have to ask for 15.”

她說：”邁克爾，我可以給你 12%，但你必須要求 15%。”

Michael still doesn’t get it. After a little back-and-forth fumbling, and a frustrated Jan telling him to “Just ask for 15,” it is finally clear to Michael what he is supposed to say. He goes:

邁克爾還是不明白。經過一番反反復復的摸索，以及沮喪的簡告訴他 “只要要求 15%”之後，邁克爾終於明白他應該說什麽了。他說

“I want 15%”

“我要 15%”

Jan, with a sigh of relief, says, “I can offer you 12%”

簡松了一口氣，說：”我可以給你 12%”。

Michael, plaintively, comes back with, “But you said 15%!” Even after it is over, he still doesn’t grasp what happened.

邁克爾哀怨地回道：”可你說的是 15%！” 即使事情已經結束，他還是不明白發生了什麽。

If it had been two Sociopaths navigating around an affair, it would have been no fun at all for Toby ((if I recall correctly, Toby is asked to leave at some point where the collapse of the due process fiction is too complete to permit the presence of a real witness — somebody correct me if I am misremembering).

如果是兩個反社會的人在戀情麻煩下，順利周旋避開，那對托比來說就一點意思都沒有了（如果我沒記錯的話，托比是在正當程序虛構崩潰到不允許真正的證人在場的某個時刻被要求離開的–如果我記錯了，誰來糾正我一下）。

大意：反社會者如果對無知者說權力語，有時候也是會有撞車危險的。

The Characteristics of Powertalk 權力語的特點

Multiple layers of meaning are not what make Powertalk unique. Irony and sarcasm are modes of layered communication available to anybody. As you’ll learn if you read the Transactional Analysis books, Gametalk is all about multiple (usually two) levels of communication. What distinguishes Powertalk is that with every word uttered, the power equation between the two speakers shifts just a little. Sometimes both gain slightly, at the expense of some poor schmuck. Sometimes one yields ground to the other. Powertalk in other words, is a consequential language.

多層含義並不是權力語的獨特之處。諷刺和挖苦是任何人都可以使用的多層次交流方式。如果你讀過《情感分析》（Transactional Analysis）一書，你就會知道，遊戲語就是一種多層次（通常是兩層）的交流方式。

權力語的與眾不同之處在於，每說一個字，兩個說話者之間的權力等式就會發生一點變化。有時，雙方都略有得益，但代價是一些可憐的笨蛋。有時，一方會讓另一方得勢。換句話說，權力語是一種後果性語言。

大意：權力語是多層次的，遊戲語也有兩層次。權力語的獨特點是說完會讓權力發生轉變。

When the Clueless or Losers talk, on the other hand, nothing really changes. Relative positions remain the same all around. Shifts happen only by accident. Even in the rare cases where exploitable information is exchanged, its value is not recognized or reflected in the exchange. Posturetalk, Babytalk and Gametalk leave power relations basically unchanged. Posturetalk and Babytalk leave things unchanged because they are, to quote Shakespeare, “full of sound and fury, signifying nothing.” Gametalk leaves power relations unchanged because its entire purpose is to help Losers put themselves and each other into safe pigeonholes that validate do-nothing life scripts.

另一方面，當 “無知者 “或 “失敗者 “說話時，什麽都不會改變。各方的相對位置保持不變。轉變只是偶然發生的。即使在極少數情況下交換了可利用的信息，其價值也不會在交換中得到認可或體現。姿態語、寶寶語和遊戲語基本上沒有改變權力關系。姿態語和寶寶語讓事情保持不變，因為引用莎士比亞的話說，它們 “充滿了聲音和憤怒，毫無意義”。遊戲語讓權力關係保持不變，因為它的全部目的就是幫助失敗者把自己和對方放進安全的鴿子籠裏，從而認可無所事事的生活腳本。

大意：姿態語、寶寶語都讓權力關係保持不變。遊戲語的目的也是讓權力關係保持不變，因為是讓失敗者之間互相認可躺平的生活。

Another way to understand the difference between Powertalk and the other languages is with a card-playing analogy. In Powertalk, you play with money (the currency is most often reality-information). In the other languages you are playing with no stakes. The most important enabling factor in being able to speak Powertalk is simply the possession of table stakes. Without it, whatever you say is Posturetalk. The only Powertalk you can speak with no table stakes is “silence.” If you are Clueless or a Loser and accidentally acquire some leverage (like when Phyllis learns of the Angela-Dwight affair), but can’t speak Powertalk, the old adage applies: a fool and his money are soon parted. As those Chester Karrass people like to say, you don’t get what you deserve; you get what you negotiate.

另一種理解權力語與其他語言之間區別的方法是用打牌來做類比。在權力語中，你玩的是錢（貨幣通常是現實信息）。而在其他語言中，您玩的是沒有籌碼的遊戲。能夠講好權力語的最重要因素就是擁有牌桌籌碼。沒有籌碼，你說什麽都是 “姿態語 “。在沒有籌碼的情況下，你唯一能說的 “權力語 “就是 “沈默 “。如果你是 “無知者 “或 “失敗者 “，意外獲得了一些籌碼（比如菲利斯知道了安吉拉-德懷特的姦情），卻不會說權力語，那麽老話說得好：”傻瓜和他的錢很快就會分開”。就像切斯特-卡拉斯（Chester Karrass）的那些談判大師喜歡說的那樣，你不會得到你應得的東西；你會得到你通過談判得到的東西。

大意：權力語是在談判，通常籌碼是現實信息。沒有籌碼的人不能說權力語，只能說姿態語。

If you’ve watched movies dedicated to the evil sorts of Sociopaths (like say Wall Street or Boiler Room) you might be under the impression that Sociopaths communicate by retreating to places where the Clueless and the Losers can’t hear them. Out there on the golf course, or in private dining rooms in exclusive restaurants, you might think, they let their guard down and speak bluntly, with liberal cursing and openly cruel jokes about non-Sociopaths.

如果你看過專門描寫反社會者邪惡一面的電影（比如《華爾街》或《鍋爐房》），你可能會有這樣的印象：反社會者的交流方式是躲到那些無知者和失敗者聽不見的地方。在外面的高爾夫球場上，或者在高級餐廳的私人包廂裏，你可能會認為，他們會放松警惕，直言不諱，對非反社會者放肆咒罵，公開開殘忍的玩笑。

You couldn’t be more wrong. That sort of private candor is actually a type of aggressive Posturetalk prevalent among the Clueless in the more superficially macho (finance) or actually dangerous industries. A fine example is Joe Pesci’s Clueless (in the Mafia context) character, Tommy de Vito, in Goodfellas. I don’t have time to analyze this movie, but a word to the wise should be sufficient: the true Sociopaths in the movie, like the characters played by Robert de Niro or Ray Liotta, never trap themselves in a corner with their own posturing: “I’m funny how? I mean, funny like I’m a clown, I amuse you? I make you laugh…I’m here to fuckin’ amuse you? What do you mean funny, funny how?” Yes, Tommy shoots the waiter (another Clueless Posturetalker who unwisely sasses a Clueless guy with a gun), but that still counts as “sound and fury, signifying nothing.”

你大錯特錯了。這種私下裏的坦率其實是一種咄咄逼人的姿態語，在那些表面上比較大男子主義（金融業）或實際上比較危險的行業裏，這種姿態語在無知者中間非常普遍。喬-佩西（Joe Pesci）在《四海好家夥》（Goodfellas）中扮演的 “無知者 “（在黑手黨背景下）角色湯米-德-維托（Tommy de Vito）就是一個很好的例子。我沒有時間分析這部電影，但我只點你一水就足夠了：電影中真正的反社會者，就像羅伯特-德尼羅或雷-利奧塔扮演的角色一樣，從來不會用自己的姿態把自己困在角落裏： “我怎麽搞笑了？我是說，像我是個小醜一樣有趣，我逗你開心？我逗你笑……我他媽就是來逗你玩的？什麽叫好笑，怎麽好笑？” 是的，湯米槍殺了服務生（另一個無知者的姿態語說話者，他不明智地用槍辱罵了一個無知者的人），但這仍然算作 “聲色俱厲，毫無意義”。

大意：影劇作品中，邪惡的反社會者都會躲起來交流，私底下會坦率殘忍地說出咄咄逼人的狠話，但其實這些是無知者才會做的事。

The bulk of Sociopath communication takes places out in the open, coded in Powertalk, right in the presence of non-Sociopaths (a decent 101 level example of this is in Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix, when Hermoine is the only one who realizes that Prof. Umbridge’s apparently bland and formulaic speech is a Powertalk speech challenging Dumbledore). As the David-Jim example shows, Sociopaths are in fact more careful in private.

大部分反社會者的交流都是在公開場合進行的，用權力語編碼，就在非反社會者面前（《哈利-波特與鳳凰社》中有一個 101 入門級的好例子，當時妙麗是唯一一個意識到恩不里居表面上平淡無奇、公式化的講話是向鄧不利多挑戰的權力語的人）。正如戴維-吉姆的例子所示，反社會者在私下裏其實更加小心謹慎。

大意：其實反社會者都公開交流，因為權力語經過編碼，能聽懂的人不多。

Why? Both examples illustrate the reasons clearly: for Sociopaths, conditions of conflict of interest and moral hazard are not exceptional. They are normal, everyday situations. To function effectively they must constantly maintain and improve their position in the ecosystem of other Sociopaths, protecting themselves, competing, forming alliances, trading favors and building trust. Above all they must be wary of Sociopaths with misaligned agendas, and protect themselves in basic ways before attempting things like cooperation. They never lower their masks. In fact they are their masks. There is nothing beneath.

為什麽呢？這兩個例子都清楚地說明了原因：對於反社會者來說，利益衝突和道德風險的情況並不特殊。它們是正常的日常情況。為了有效地發揮作用，他們必須不斷保持和提高自己在其他反社會者生態系統中的地位，保護自己，參與競爭，結成聯盟，交換恩惠，建立信任。最重要的是，他們必須警惕那些目的不一致的反社會者，在嘗試合作等事情之前，先用基本的方法保護自己。他們從不放下面具。事實上，他們就是他們的面具。下面什麽都沒有。

大意：反社會者將利益衝突、道德風險都看作普通日常情況。所以不管私底下或公開，他們隨時警覺、持續練習，說的每句話都是權力語，保護自己、交換權力關係。

So effective Sociopaths stick with steadfast discipline to the letter of the law, internal and external, because the stupidest way to trip yourself up is in the realm of rules where the Clueless and Losers get to be judges and jury members. What they violate is its spirit, by taking advantage of its ambiguities. Whether this makes them evil or good depends on the situation. That’s a story for another day. Good Sociopaths operate by what they personally choose as a higher morality, in reaction to what they see as the dangers, insanities and stupidities of mob morality. Evil Sociopaths are merely looking for a quick, safe buck. Losers and the Clueless, of course, avoid individual moral decisions altogether.

因此，有效的反社會者會堅定不移地遵守法律條文，無論是內部的還是外部的，因為最愚蠢的絆倒自己的方式就是在規則的領域裏，讓無知者和失敗者成為法官和陪審團成員。他們違反的是法律的精神，利用法律的模糊性。至於他們是邪惡還是善良，這要視情況而定。那是後話了。好的反社會者遵循他們個人選擇的更高道德，以應對他們所認為的暴民道德的危險、瘋狂和愚蠢。邪惡的反社會者只是為了快速、安全地賺錢。當然，失敗者和無知者則完全回避個人的道德抉擇。

大意：反社會者不會違反法律條文，只會違反法律精神。善良反社會者是出於面對暴民的自保，邪惡反社會者是出於自私利益。失敗者和無知者則是都不違反，會願意完全遵守公眾道德。

Do watch Wall Street or Boiler Room if you haven’t by the way; appropriately, in an Office Halloween party, Ryan comes dressed as Gordon Gekko, the Michael Douglas character in Wall Street and archetypal modern finance Sociopath. Goodfellas is great fun of course, but not as easily translated to non-criminal workplaces. It is based on a true story, as is a more recent Mafia story, Making Jack Falcone. Though distant from our worlds, criminal worlds have the one advantage that they do not need to maintain the fiction that the organization is not pathological, so they are revealing to study.

順便說一句，如果你還沒看過《華爾街》或《鍋爐房》，那就去看看吧；在一次辦公室萬聖節派對上，瑞安打扮成《華爾街》中邁克爾-道格拉斯飾演的戈登-蓋科（Gordon Gekko）和現代金融反社會者的典型形象，真是恰如其分。當然，《四好好家夥》非常有趣，但並不容易被翻譯到非犯罪的工作場所。它改編自一個真實的故事，就像最近的黑手黨故事《傑克-法爾科內》（Making Jack Falcone）一樣。儘管與我們的世界相距甚遠，但犯罪世界有一個優勢，那就是他們不需要維持組織並非病態的虛構，因此研究起來很有啟發性。

大意：犯罪世界的組織不需要表面上隱藏自己的病態，在研究能揭露更多。

How Not to Learn Powertalk: Toy Guns and Treacle 如何不學習權力語： 玩具槍和糖衣炮彈

Assuming you have table stakes, how do you learn to speak Powertalk as fluently as accomplished Sociopaths? That’s hard, and I’ll provide a couple of pointers at the end. It is illuminating though, to look at a couple of examples of how not to acquire the skill. People who try earnestly to learn Powertalk from recipe books end up merely expanding their Posturetalk vocabulary. There are two good examples in The Office. I’ll call these vocabularies Toy Guns and Treacle. These are vocabularies within Posturetalk that reflect Clueless attempts to mimic Powertalk, so this is actually a bit of a preview of Posturetalk.

假設你有牌桌籌碼，那麽如何才能學會像成功的反社會者那樣流利地說權力語呢？這很難，我將在最後提供一些建議。不過，看幾個不掌握這項技能的例子還是很有啟發性的。那些認真嘗試從口訣書中學習權力語的人，最終只是擴大了他們的姿態語詞匯量。《辦公室》中有兩個很好的例子。我把它們分別叫做 “玩具槍”（Toy Guns）和 “糖漿”（Treacle）。這些都是姿態語中的詞匯，反映了無知者試圖模仿權力語的嘗試，所以這實際上也算是姿態語的一點預覽。

大意：無知者學不會權力語，試圖模仿，結果變成姿態語中的玩具槍與糖漿。

Toy Guns 玩具槍

Toy Guns is the vocabulary of empty machismo.

玩具槍（Toy Guns）是空洞的大男子主義詞匯。

The example is again from the Michael-Darryl salary negotiation. Michael prints off negotiation guidelines from Wikipedia and attempts to use a series of recommended formulaic tactics.

這個例子還是來自邁克爾-達裏爾（Michael-Darryl）的薪資談判。邁克爾從維基百科上打印了談判指南，並試圖使用一系列推薦的公式化策略。

First he tries switching chairs and rooms to disorient Darryl. He merely disorients himself.

首先，他嘗試調換椅子和房間來迷惑達裏爾。他只是讓自己失去了方向。

Next he tries to follow a rule to “not be the first to speak.” Sadly, he can’t stand the tension and, oblivious to the irony, breaks the silence with “I will not be the first to speak.” At which point Darryl calmly comes back with, “Alright, I can start.”

接下來，他試圖遵循 “不第一個發言 “的規則。可悲的是，他無法忍受這種緊張氣氛，無視其中的諷刺意味，用 “我不會第一個發言 “打破了沈默。這時，達裏爾平靜地回道：”好吧，可以由我開始。”

Finally, the abject performance reaches its nadir when Michael forces Darryl to adhere to the ritual of writing down his opening offer and sliding it, folded, across the table. When Darryl attempts to just hand it to him, Michael insists on the sliding. Darryl humors him (the basic motivation in Babytalk is “humor the baby” — we’ll see why next time).

最後，當邁克爾強迫達裏爾遵守儀式，寫下他的開價，並將其折疊後滑過桌子時，這種卑劣的表演達到了最低點。當達裏爾試圖直接遞給他時，邁克爾堅持要滑動。達裏爾幽默地回應了他（寶寶語的基本動機是 “用幽默回應寶寶”–我們下次再看為什麽）。

大意：玩具槍大概就是虛張聲勢。

Treacle 糖漿

Treacle is a vocabulary drawn from apparently win-win/play nice frameworks, but deployed with adversarial intent.

糖漿是一個從表面上的雙贏/友好框架中提取的詞匯，但在使用時卻帶有敵對意圖。

The example is from a Sociopathy sideshow: Angela’s fiefdom, the Party Planning Committee. In the episode in question, Phyllis attempts to use textbook “nice” manipulation methods (such as “active listening” and “effective feedback”), which she learns from some unidentified training material. When Phyllis repeatedly screws up (getting a sign printed wrong, and then failing to get forks and knives along with spoons), Angela blows up. Phyllis tries to manipulate Angela into “effective feedback” mode by asking the formulaic question, “How does that make you feel?” Angela, with icy sarcasm, explains that she is feeling “angry” because Phyllis is “stupid.” She then proceeds to explain (icy sarcasm continuing) what forks and knives are.

這個例子來自一個反社會的小節目： 安吉拉的領地–派對計劃委員會。在這一集中，菲利斯試圖使用教科書上的 “善意 “操縱方法（如 “積極傾聽 “和 “有效反饋 “），這是她從一些不知名的培訓材料中學來的。當菲利斯一再搞砸時（印錯了一個標誌，然後又沒拿到刀叉和勺子），安吉拉頓時火冒三丈。菲利斯試圖操縱安吉拉進入 “有效反饋 “模式，提出了一個公式化的問題：”這讓你感覺如何？”安吉拉冷嘲熱諷地解釋說，她感到 “憤怒 “是因為菲利斯 “愚蠢 “。然後她繼續解釋（繼續冷嘲熱諷）什麽是刀叉。

For those of you unfamiliar with this stuff, the way “effective feedback” is supposed to work is that the criticizer points out the specific behavior in question without judgment, and then explains how the behavior made him/her feel. Preferably when the incident is immediate and fresh. It is supposed to lead away from the toxic business of labeling others and evoking defenses. Great in theory for people whose interests are aligned.

對於不熟悉這些東西的人來說，”有效反饋 “的工作方式應該是，批評者不帶評判地指出有關的具體行為，然後解釋這種行為給他/她帶來的感受。最好是在事件剛剛發生的時候。這樣做的目的是為了避免給他人貼標籤和喚起防衛的有毒行為。理論上，這對利益一致的人來說很好。

But when a bad-faith incompetent like Phyllis attempts to use the technique to deflect a tirade from an angry Sociopath with no reason to be nice, initiating the “effective feedback” psychology parlor game is about the same as putting on a sign labeled “Kick Me!”

但當像菲利斯這樣的不稱職的人試圖用這種技巧來轉移一個憤怒的反社會者的長篇說教時，發起 “有效反饋 “的心理學會客室遊戲，就像掛上一個標有 “踢我！”的牌子一樣。

Predictably, Phyllis got kicked.

可想而知，菲利斯被踢了。

大意：糖漿是想要笑裡藏刀，但是面對沒有共信基礎的對手，通常會被識破、不買帳。

Why the Textbook Material Fails 教科書材料為何失敗

So what is going wrong here? Why can’t you learn Sociopath tactics from a book or Wikipedia? It is not that the tactics themselves are misguided, but that their application by non-Sociopaths is usually useless, for three reasons.

那麽，這裏到底出了什麽問題？為什麽你不能從書本或維基百科中學到反社會者的策略？這並不是說這些策略本身是錯誤的，而是說非反社會者運用這些策略通常是無用的，原因有三。

大意：教科書寫的都沒錯，但是非反社會者不會用。

The first is that you have to decide what tactics to use and when*,* based on a real sense of the relative power and alignment of interests with the other party, which the Losers and Clueless typically lack. This real-world information is what makes for tactical surprise. Otherwise your application of even the most subtle textbook tactics can be predicted and easily countered by any Sociopath who has also read the same book. Null information advantage.

首先，你必須在真正了解對方的相對實力和利益一致的基礎上，決定在什麽時候使用什麽戰術，而失敗者和無知者通常缺乏這種能力。這種真實世界的信息是戰術出奇制勝的關鍵。否則，即使是最微妙的教科書戰術，任何反社會者都能預測並輕松反擊，因為他們也讀過同樣的書。無效信息優勢。

大意：只要對手也讀過同樣的書，就沒有信息優勢。而且反社會者更能活用。

The second reason is that tactics make sense only in the context of an entire narrative (including mutual assessments of personality, strengths, weaknesses and history) of a given interpersonal relationship. The Clueless have no sense of narrative rationality, and the Losers are too trapped in their own stories to play to other scripts. Both the Clueless and Losers are too self-absorbed to put in much work developing accurate and usable mental models of others. The result is one-size-fits-all-situations tactical choices which are easily anticipated and deflected.

第二個原因是，戰術只有在特定人際關係的整體敘述（包括對個性、優點、缺點和歷史的相互評估）中才有意義。無知者沒有敘事合理性的意識，而失敗者則被自己的故事所困，無法按照其他劇本行事。無知者和失敗者都太自我陶醉，不願意花大力氣去建立準確可用的他人心智模型。其結果是，”一刀切 “式的戰術選擇很容易被預料到，也很容易被回避。

大意：太自我陶醉於故事裡的人，做不到揣摩他人的心理，都以自我出發，以至於選擇的戰術太容易被人看破。無知者幻想自己上進，失敗者幻想自己追求幸福。

And the third and most important reason of course, is that your moves have to be backed up by appropriate bets using your table stakes, exposing you to real risks and rewards. A good way to remember this is to think of Powertalk as decisions about what verbal tactics to use when, and with what. The answer to with what is usually a part of your table-stakes. The stuff you are revealing and risking. If you cannot answer with what? you are posturing. You are not speaking Powertalk. In the Jim-Wallace example, with what was Jim’s superior knowledge of the Michael-Jan story.

當然，第三個也是最重要的一個原因是，你的行動必須以適當的賭注為支撐，使用你的牌桌籌碼，讓你面臨真正的風險和回報。記住這一點的一個好方法是，將 “權力語 “視為決定何時使用何種口頭戰術以及用什麽的決策。用什麽的答案通常是你牌桌籌碼的一部分。你所透露的信息和所冒的風險。如果你不能回答 “用什麽 “，那你就是在裝腔作勢。你說的不是權力語。在吉姆-華萊士的例子中，吉姆對邁克爾-簡的故事有更深入的了解就是他的 “用什麽 “。

Bottomline: you cannot learn Powertalk from books. Which leads to the question: is there any way to learn it at all?

一句話：你無法從書本上學到權力語。 這就引出了一個問題：到底有沒有辦法學會它？

大意：賭上的籌碼通常是信息，思考過切身的風險跟回報，才真的懂權力語。

The Art of Powertalk 權力語的藝術

Even in the hands of fluent Powertalkers with an understanding of their own credibility, command of the language is simply not a formulaic or procedural skill. It is a thinking skill. We’ve learned so far that it is a very thoughtful and calibrated use of language based on an accurate and current sense of your actual power*.* It is a game played with real stakes. Just knowing whatever few rules exist is of no real use, it’s merely a basic condition of participation.

即使是對自己的可信度了如指掌的 “權力語 “高手，掌握這種語言也不是一種公式化或程序化的技能。它是一種思維技巧。到目前為止，我們已經了解到，這是一種基於對自身實際能力的準確和最新感知，經過深思熟慮和校準後使用的語言。它是一種有實際賭注的遊戲。僅僅知道一些規則是沒有實際用處的，這只是參與遊戲的基本條件。

大意：權力語不能靠公式化程序化掌握，隨時都需要思考跟校準，條件瞬息萬變，自己當下的權力有多少要抓得準，還要考慮如何在談判中用得好。

There is a reason I used an analogy to vocabularies in the last section. Remember those kids who earnestly memorized big word lists for their SATs and the GRE? Notice any of them winning literature Nobel Prizes? Vocabulary expansion efforts can at best put the finishing touches on organically acquired language skills. There is no shortcut to organic language acquisition; reading well-written stuff and writing constantly is the only way. The same holds for Powertalk. You learn through real Powertalk conversations with other Sociopaths. Betting real stakes (information, credibility, labor and literal dollar money). You get played for a sucker a few times along the way before you wise up. Even if you are a good Sociopath, you learn to swallow your distaste and occasionally play hardball when you have to.

我在上一節中使用了詞匯量的比喻，這是有原因的。還記得那些為了參加 SAT 和 GRE 考試而認真背誦大量單詞表的孩子嗎？他們中有人獲得諾貝爾文學獎嗎？擴充詞匯量的努力充其量只能對有機習得的語言技能起到畫龍點睛的作用。語言的有機習得沒有捷徑可走，只有不斷地讀好書、寫好文章。權力語也是如此。您可以通過與其他反社會者進行真實的權力語對話來學習。押上真正的籌碼（信息、信譽、勞動力和字面意義上的美元）。一路上你會被耍得團團轉好幾次，然後才會清醒過來。即使你是一個優秀的反社會者，你也要學會忍氣吞聲，在必要的時候偶爾來硬的。

大意：權力語需要親自實踐，學以致用，也有其代價，過程並不輕鬆。

But if you do have the table stakes to join important conversations, and the mental toughness to play risk-and-reward games with every conversational move, there are a couple of skills worth practicing.

但是，如果你有加入重要談話的牌桌籌碼，有在每一次談話中都玩風險與回報遊戲的精神韌性，那麽有幾種技能值得練習。

One skill is storytelling, and I covered aspects of this briefly before (Bargaining with Your Right Brain). With enough practice (a LOT), this gives you big-picture control over conversations.

其中一項技能就是講故事，我以前曾簡要介紹過這方面的內容（《與右腦討價還價》）。通過足夠的練習（大量的練習），這可以讓你在對話中擁有大局觀。

Low-level utterance-by-utterance control is much harder, and the one thing you cannot do is engineer 7-8 meanings and calibrated amounts of power and leverage into every line you utter, through careful word choice. You don’t have the luxury of minutes or hours between responses (you can do that over email though). In most conversations, you have tenths of a second per response. In that time you must steer the tempo of the conversation — its rhythms, emotional subtext and energy level — to affect power equations the way you want. Chapter 3 of my book Tempo covers these things briefly.

低層次的逐字逐句控制要難得多，你無法做到的一件事就是通過精心選詞，將 7-8 種含義和經過校準的力量和杠桿作用融入你的每一句話中。你沒有幾分鐘或幾小時的間隔時間（不過你可以通過電子郵件做到這一點）。在大多數對話中，每次回復只有十分之一秒的時間。在這段時間裏，你必須控制對話的節奏–它的韻律、情感潛臺詞和能量水平–以你想要的方式影響力量方程式。我在《節奏》一書中的第 3 章簡要介紹了這些內容。

By the way, for those of you who have the stomach for a rather academic look at organizational languages (what you could call silo or guild languages rather than power languages), try this paper: On Languages for Dynamic Resource Scheduling Problems, by Warren Powell.

順便說一句，如果你們有興趣對組織語言（可稱為筒倉語言或行會語言，而非權力語言）進行學術探討，不妨試試這篇論文： 論動態資源調度問題的語言》，作者沃倫-鮑威爾（Warren Powell）。

大意：去看這些書，其中的技巧能幫你把權力語用得好。

本次就到此為止。下一部分，我們將從杜寧-克魯格效應（Duning-Kruger Effect）的視角來審視 “無知者 “的世界。

The Gervais Principle P1

熱爾韋原理 1

The Gervais Principle, Or The Office According to “The Office”

October 7, 2009 By Venkatesh Rao

原文網址:
https://www.ribbonfarm.com/2009/10/07/the-gervais-principle-or-the-office-according-to-the-office/

My neighbor introduced me to The Office back in 2005. Since then, I’ve watched every episode of both the British and American versions. I’ve watched the show obsessively because I’ve been unable to figure out what makes it so devastatingly effective, and elevates it so far above the likes of Dilbert and Office Space.

2005 年，我的鄰居向我介紹了《辦公室風雲》。從那時起，我看完了英國版和美國版的每一集。我之所以癡迷於這部劇，是因為我一直弄不明白是什麽讓它具有如此巨大的殺傷力，讓它遠遠超越了《呆伯特》和《辦公室空間》。

Until now, that is. Now, after four years, I’ve finally figured the show out. The Office is not a random series of cynical gags aimed at momentarily alleviating the existential despair of low-level grunts. It is a fully realized theory of management that falsifies 83.8% of the business section of the bookstore. The theory begins with Hugh MacLeod’s well-known cartoon, Company Hierarchy (below), and its cornerstone is something I will call The Gervais Principle, which supersedes both the Peter Principle and its successor, The Dilbert Principle. Outside of the comic aisle, the only major and significant works consistent with the Gervais Principle are The Organization Man and Images of Organization.

直到現在，就是這樣。四年後的今天，我終於弄明白了這部劇。《辦公室》並不是一連串冷嘲熱諷的插科打諢，其目的也不只是為了暫時緩解基層員工的絕望情緒。它是一個完全實現了的管理理論，其正確性贏過書店中商業區 83.8% 的內容。 該理論始於休-麥克勞德（Hugh MacLeod）的著名漫畫《公司等級制度》（下圖），其基石我稱之為“熱爾韋原理”（The Gervais Principle），它取代了“彼得原理”及其後繼者“呆伯特法則”。在漫畫領域之外，唯一符合熱爾韋原則的重要作品是《組織人》和《組織形象》。

大意：《辦公室》當中可以解讀出一種先進的管理理論，叫“熱爾韋原理”

I’ll need to lay just a little bit of groundwork (lest you think this whole post is a riff based on cartoons) before I can get to the principle and my interpretation of The Office. I’ll be basing this entire article on the American version of the show, which is more fully developed than the original British version, though the original is perhaps more satisfyingly bleak. Keep in mind that this is an interpretation of The Office as management science; the truth in the art. Literary/artistic critics don’t really seem to get it. I’ll have some passing comments to offer on the comedy and art of it all, but this is primarily about the truths revealed by the show, pursued with Dwight-like earnestness.

在談及此原理和我對《辦公室》的理解之前，我需要先做一點鋪墊（免得你以為這整篇文章都是根據動畫片改編的）。我的整篇文章都將以美版《辦公室》為基礎，美版《辦公室》比英國原版《辦公室》發展得更全面，儘管英國原版《辦公室》也許在冷峻上更令人滿意。請記住，這是將《辦公室》解讀為管理科學；藝術中的真理。 文學/藝術評論家似乎並不真正理解這一點。我會順便就其中的喜劇性和藝術性發表一些評論，但這主要是關於該劇所揭示的真理，以德懷特式的認真態度追求的真理。

大意：我會從管理科學的角度來解釋他

From The Whyte School to The Gervais Principle 從懷特學派到熱爾韋原理

Hugh MacLeod’s cartoon is a pitch-perfect symbol of an unorthodox school of management based on the axiom that organizations don’t suffer pathologies; they are intrinsically pathological constructs. Idealized organizations are not perfect. They are perfectly pathological. So while most most management literature is about striving relentlessly towards an ideal by executing organization theories completely, this school, which I’ll call the Whyte school, would recommend that you do the bare minimum organizing to prevent chaos, and then stop. Let a natural, if declawed, individualist Darwinism operate beyond that point. The result is the MacLeod hierarchy. It may be horrible, but like democracy, it is the best you can do.

休-麥克勞德（Hugh MacLeod）的漫畫是非正統管理學派的完美象徵，該學派基於這樣一個公理：組織並不會遭受病態的危害，它們本質上是病態的構造。理想化的組織並不完美。它們是病態得很完美。因此，當大多數管理文獻都在通過完全執行組織理論來為理想而不懈努力時，這個學派，我稱之為懷特學派，則建議你做最基本的組織工作，以防止混亂，然後停止。讓一種自然的（如果被剝奪利爪的）個人主義達爾文主義在這一點之外發揮作用。這就是麥克勞德等級制度。這可能很可怕，但就像民主一樣，這是你能做的最好的事情。

大意：組織自己就很病態，所以麥克勞德學派不會很用力去管理它，而是順其自然發展成麥克勞德金字塔。

The Sociopath (capitalized) layer comprises the Darwinian/Protestant Ethic will-to-power types who drive an organization to function despite itself. The Clueless layer is what Whyte called the “Organization Man,” but the archetype inhabiting the middle has evolved a good deal since Whyte wrote his book (in the fifties). The Losers are not social losers (as in the opposite of “cool”), but people who have struck bad bargains economically – giving up capitalist striving for steady paychecks. I am not making this connection up. Consider this passage from OM:

反社會者（大寫）層由達爾文主義/新教倫理的權力意志類型組成，他們推動一個組織不顧自身的運作。無知層就是懷特所說的“組織人”，但自從懷特寫下這本書（50 年代）以來，處在中間層的原型已經發生了很大變化。“失敗者”並不是社會的失敗者（與“酷”相反），而是在經濟上討價還價失敗的人–他們追求穩定薪水，放棄了成為資本家。這種聯繫不是我捏造的。請看《組織人》的這段話：

  • Of all organization men, the true executive is the one who remains most suspicious of The Organization. If there is one thing that characterizes him, it is a fierce desire to control his own destiny and, deep down, he resents yielding that control to The Organization, no matter how velvety its grip… he wants to dominate, not be dominated…Many people from the great reaches of middle management can become true believers in The Organization…But the most able are not vouchsafed this solace.

    在所有組織人員中，真正的執行者是最懷疑組織的人。如果說他有什麽特點的話，那就是強烈渴望掌握自己的命運，而在內心深處，他反感將這種控制權交給組織，無論組織的控制力有多麽天鵝絨般柔軟……他想要主宰，而不是被主宰……許多中層管理人員都能成為組織的忠實信徒……但最有能力的人卻得不到這種慰藉。

    大意：上層的反社會者會推動組織能自主運作，但是本身排斥受到組織主宰，不信任組織。中層的“組織人”是忠實信徒。下層的失敗者滿足於穩定的薪水。

Back then, Whyte was extremely pessimistic. He saw signs that in the struggle for dominance between the Sociopaths (whom he admired as the ones actually making the organization effective despite itself) and the middle-management Organization Man, the latter was winning. He was wrong, but not in the way you’d think. The Sociopaths defeated the Organization Men and turned them into The Clueless not by reforming the organization, but by creating a meta-culture of Darwinism in the economy: one based on job-hopping, mergers, acquisitions, layoffs, cataclysmic reorganizations, outsourcing, unforgiving start-up ecosystems, and brutal corporate raiding. In this terrifying meta-world of the Titans, the Organization Man became the Clueless Man. Today, any time an organization grows too brittle, bureaucratic and disconnected from reality, it is simply killed, torn apart and cannibalized, rather than reformed. The result is the modern creative-destructive life cycle of the firm, which I’ll call the MacLeod Life Cycle.

當時，懷特非常悲觀。他看到的跡象是，在反社會者（他欽佩反社會者，因為是他們讓組織真正能自主發揮了效力）和中層管理者“組織人”（Organization Man）之間的統治權爭奪戰中，後者正在獲勝。他錯了，但不是你想像的那樣。反社會者打敗了“組織人”，並把他們變成了“無知者”，他們不是通過改革組織，而是通過在經濟中創造一種達爾文主義的元文化：一種基於跳槽、兼併、收購、裁員、災難性重組、外包、無情的創業生態系統和殘酷的企業掠奪的元文化。在這個可怕的“泰坦”元世界裏，“組織人”變成了“無知者”。如今，只要一個組織變得過於脆弱、官僚和脫離現實，它就會被扼殺、撕裂和蠶食，而不是進行改革。這就是現代企業的創造性-破壞性生命周期，我稱之為“麥克勞德生命周期”。

大意：過去的組織會經由改革，保持有效運作，同時讓領導層能壓過中間組織人，掌控統治權。現在則根本不會改革，反社會者會主動扼殺脆弱的組織，回收其中的資源再去創造新的組織，這個循環過程中，中間層只能受到擺弄，淪為無知者。

A Sociopath with an idea recruits just enough Losers to kick off the cycle. As it grows it requires a Clueless layer to turn it into a controlled reaction rather than a runaway explosion. Eventually, as value hits diminishing returns, both the Sociopaths and Losers make their exits, and the Clueless start to dominate. Finally, the hollow brittle shell collapses on itself and anything of value is recycled by the sociopaths according to meta-firm logic.

有想法的反社會者招募足夠多的失敗者來啟動循環。隨著循環的發展，需要一個“無知層”將其轉化為受控反應，而不是失控爆炸。最終，隨著價值回報的減少，反社會者和失敗者都會退出，無知者開始佔據主導地位。最後，空心脆殼自行坍塌，任何有價值的東西都會被反社會者按照元公司邏輯回收利用。

大意：用核子反應比喻，中間無知層是用來輔助組織的管理，但是晚年的公司收益遞減，上層的領導跟下層真正做事的人會離開，同時中間管理層冗員會留著，然後公司倒閉進入回收週期(看圖片中各層人數的比例)。

MacLeod’s Loser layer had me puzzled for a long time, because I was interpreting it in cultural terms: the kind of person you call a “loser.” While some may be losers in that sense too, they are primarily losers in the economic sense: those who have, for various reasons, made (or been forced to make) a bad economic bargain. They’ve given up some potential for long-term economic liberty (as capitalists) for short-term economic stability. Traded freedom for a paycheck in short. They actually produce, but are not compensated in proportion to the value they create (since their compensation is set by Sociopaths operating under conditions of serious moral hazard). They mortgage their lives away, and hope to die before their money runs out. The good news is that Losers have two ways out, which we’ll get to later: turning Sociopath or turning into bare-minimum performers. The Losers destined for cluelessness do not have a choice.

麥克勞德的“失敗者”層讓我困惑了很久，因為我是從文化角度來理解的：你稱之為“輸家”的那類人。雖然有些人也可能是這種意義上的失敗者，但他們主要是經濟意義上的失敗者：那些由於各種原因而做出（或被迫做出）了糟糕的經濟交易的人。他們（作為資本家）放棄了一些長期經濟自由的潛力，以換取短期的經濟穩定。簡而言之，就是用自由換取薪水。他們實際上在生產，但卻得不到與他們創造的價值成正比的報酬（因為他們的報酬是由反社會者在嚴重的道德風險條件下制定的）。他們把自己的生命抵押出去，希望在錢花光之前死去。好消息是，失敗者有兩條出路，我們稍後會講到：要麽變成反社會者，要麽變成最低限度的工作表現者。注定轉為無知者的失敗者則沒有選擇的餘地。

大意：失敗者的工作報酬不成正比，用自由換取薪水穩定。失敗者有三種可能路線，反社會者、上班摸魚、中層的無知者。

Based on the MacLeod lifecycle, we can also separate the three layers based on the timing of their entry and exit into organizations. The Sociopaths enter and exit organizations at will, at any stage, and do whatever it takes to come out on top. The contribute creativity in early stages of a organization’s life, neurotic leadership in the middle stages, and cold-bloodedness in the later stages, where they drive decisions like mergers, acquisitions and layoffs that others are too scared or too compassionate to drive. They are also the ones capable of equally impersonally exploiting a young idea for growth in the beginning, killing one good idea to concentrate resources on another at maturity, and milking an end-of-life idea through harvest-and-exit market strategies.

根據麥克勞德的生命周期，我們還可以根據他們進入和退出組織的時間來區分這三個層次。反社會者會在任何階段隨意進入和退出組織，並不惜一切代價脫穎而出。他們在組織生命的早期階段貢獻創造力，在中期階段貢獻神經質的領導力，而在後期階段則貢獻冷血，他們會推動兼併、收購和裁員等決策，而其他人則因為過於恐懼或過於同情而無法推動這些決策。他們還能以同樣不近人情的方式，在開始階段利用一個年輕的想法促進發展，在成熟階段扼殺一個好的想法，將資源集中到另一個想法上，並通過收獲和退出市場戰略榨乾一個壽終正寢的想法。

大意：反社會者擁有冷血的優勢，在早期中期晚期階段都能擔任要角，利用發揮想法跟資源，目標在搾取利益。反社會者加入跟退出公司都比較自由。

The Losers like to feel good about their lives. They are the happiness seekers, rather than will-to-power players, and enter and exit reactively, in response to the meta-Darwinian trends in the economy. But they have no more loyalty to the firm than the Sociopaths. They do have a loyalty to individual people, and a commitment to finding fulfillment through work when they can, and coasting when they cannot.

失敗者喜歡對自己的生活感覺良好。他們是追求幸福的人，而不是意志力強的人，他們根據經濟中的元達爾文趨勢(※有公司倒閉、有公司新創)，被動地進入或退出。但他們對公司的忠誠度並不比反社會者高。他們會忠於特定個人，致力於在可以的時候通過工作找到成就感，在不能找到成就感的時候則碌碌無為摸魚。

大意：失敗者的目標是感覺幸福，有時候也包含工作滿足感。失敗者加入跟退出公司有一點自由。

The Clueless are the ones who lack the competence to circulate freely through the economy (unlike Sociopaths and Losers), and build up a perverse sense of loyalty to the firm, even when events make it abundantly clear that the firm is not loyal to them. To sustain themselves, they must be capable of fashioning elaborate delusions based on idealized notions of the firm — the perfectly pathological entities we mentioned. Unless squeezed out by forces they cannot resist, they hang on as long as possible, long after both Sociopaths and Losers have left (in Douglas Adams’ vicious history of our planet, humanity was founded by a spaceship full of the Clueless, sent here by scheming Sociopaths). When cast adrift in the open ocean, they are the ones most likely to be utterly destroyed.

無知者缺乏在經濟中自由流通的能力（與反社會者和失敗者不同），他們對公司建立起一種反常的忠誠感，即使事態的發展非常清楚地表明公司對他們並不忠誠。為了維持自己的生存，他們必須能夠根據理想化的公司概念–我們提到的完全病態的實體–精心設計妄想。除非被無法抗拒的力量擠壓出去，否則他們會盡可能地堅持下去，直到反社會者和失敗者都離開很久（在道格拉斯-亞當斯關於我們星球的惡毒歷史中，人類是由一艘滿載無知者的宇宙飛船建立的，而這些無知者是被詭計多端的反社會者送到這裏來的）。當漂流在茫茫大海中時，他們最有可能被徹底毀滅。

大意：無知者會抱緊一套幻想：公司很理想、自己忠誠於公司、而公司也重視他。無知者加入跟退出公司都不自由。

Which brings us to our main idea. How both the pyramid and its lifecycle are animated. The dynamics are governed by the Newton’s Law of organizations: the Gervais Principle.

這就引出了我們的主旨。金字塔及其生命周期是如何被激活的。其動態受牛頓組織定律：熱爾韋原理的支配。

The Gervais Principle and Its Consequences 熱爾韋原理及其後果

The Gervais Principle is this:

熱爾韋原理是這樣的：

  • Sociopaths, in their own best interests, knowingly promote over-performing losers into middle-management, groom under-performing losers into sociopaths, and leave the average bare-minimum-effort losers to fend for themselves.

    反社會者為了自身的最大利益，有意將表現優異的失敗者提拔為中層管理者，將表現不佳的失敗者培養成反社會者，而讓那些只付出最低限度努力的普通失敗者自生自滅。

大意：熱爾韋原理怎麼選擇提拔失敗者

The Gervais principle differs from the Peter Principle, which it superficially resembles. The Peter Principle states that all people are promoted to the level of their incompetence. It is based on the assumption that future promotions are based on past performance. The Peter Principle is wrong for the simple reason that executives aren’t that stupid, and because there isn’t that much room in an upward-narrowing pyramid. They know what it takes for a promotion candidate to perform at the to level. So if they are promoting people beyond their competence anyway, under conditions of opportunity scarcity, there must be a good reason.

熱爾韋原理與表面上相似的彼得原理不同。彼得原理認為，所有的人都會根據其不稱職的程度得到晉升。它基於這樣一個假設，即未來的晉升是基於過去的表現。彼得原理是錯誤的，原因很簡單，高管們沒那麽蠢，而且向上收窄的金字塔也沒有那麽大的空間。他們知道晉升候選人需要在哪一級別上有所表現。因此，如果在機會稀缺的條件下，他們還是要提拔超出自己能力的人，那一定是有充分理由的。

大意：彼得原理為什麼錯誤

Scott Adams, seeing a different flaw in the Peter Principle, proposed the Dilbert Principle: that companies tend to systematically promote their least-competent employees to middle management to limit the damage they can do. This again is untrue. The Gervais principle predicts the exact opposite: that the most competent ones will be promoted to middle management. Michael Scott was a star salesman before he become a Clueless middle manager. The least competent employees (but not all of them — only certain enlightened incompetents) will be promoted not to middle management, but fast-tracked through to senior management. To the Sociopath level.

斯科特-亞當斯（Scott Adams）看到了“彼得原理”的另一個缺陷，提出了“呆伯特法則”：公司傾向於有計劃地將能力最差的員工提拔為中層管理人員，以限制他們可能造成的損害。這同樣不符合事實。熱爾韋原理的預測恰恰相反：能力最強的員工會被提拔到中層管理崗位。邁克爾-斯科特（Michael Scott）在成為無知的中層管理者之前，曾是一名明星推銷員。能力最差的員工（但不是全部，只有某些開竅的無能者）不會被提拔為中層管理人員，而是快速晉升為高級管理人員。晉升到反社會者級別。

大意：呆伯特法則為什麼錯誤

And in case you are wondering, the unenlightened under-performers get fired.

如果你想知道，那些不開竅的表現不佳者會被解雇。

Let me illustrate the logic and implications of the principle with examples from the show.

讓我用節目中的例子來說明這一原則的邏輯和含義。

The Career of the Clueless 無知者的職業生涯

In Season Three, the Dunder-Mifflin executives decide to merge the Stamford and Scranton branches, laying off much of the latter, including Michael Scott. His counterpart, the Sociopath Stamford branch manager, whose promotion is the premise of the re-org, opportunistically leverages his impending promotion into an executive position at a competitor, leaving the c0mpany in disarray. The Dunder-Mifflin executives, forced to deal with the fallout, cynically play out the now-illogical re-org anyway, shutting down Stamford and leaving Michael with the merged branch instead. The executives (David Wallace and Jan Levinson-Gould) are completely aware of Michael’s utter incompetence. Their calculations are obvious: giving Michael the expanded branch allows them to claim short-term success and buy time to maneuver out of having to personally suffer longer-term consequences.

在第三季中，Dunder-Mifflin 公司的高管們決定合併斯坦福德和斯克蘭頓兩家分部，解雇後者的大部分員工，包括邁克爾-斯科特（Michael Scott）。他的同儕競爭對手、反社會的斯坦福德分部經理的升職是重組的前提，但他卻投機取巧，利用自己即將升職的機會，在競爭對手那裏擔任了高管職位，使公司陷入混亂。Dunder-Mifflin 公司的高管們迫於壓力，無奈地進行了不合邏輯的重組，關閉了斯坦福，把邁克爾留在了合併後的分部。高管們（大衛-華萊士和簡-萊文森-古爾德）完全清楚邁克爾的無能。他們的算盤打得很明顯：把擴大後的分部交給邁克爾，既可以獲得短期的成功，又可以爭取時間，避免個人承擔長期的後果。

大意：劇情中，另外一間區域經理預定會成為兩邊合併後的主管，但是他利用這點當作籌碼談判，跳槽去了別的公司的高管職位。邁克爾本來會被解僱的，現在Dunder-Mifflin 的高管被迫留下他當主管，但其實是算計的一部份。

Jim’s remark on the drama is revealing. Comparing Michael to his exiting sociopath peer he says: “Whatever you say about Michael, he would never have done something like this,” a testament to Michael’s determinedly deluded loyalty to the company that will never be loyal to him. We can safely assume that Michael’s previous promotion to regional manager occurred under similar circumstances of callous short-term calculations by sociopaths.

吉姆對這場鬧劇的評論很有啟發性。他將邁克爾與他的反社會同行進行了比較，他說：“無論你怎麽說邁克爾，他都不會做出這樣的事。”這證明了邁克爾對公司的忠誠是堅定的、自欺欺人的，而公司永遠不會對他忠誠。我們可以有把握地假設，邁克爾之前晉升為區域經理也是在類似的情況下發生的，即反社會者冷酷無情的短期算計。

大意：跳槽的那位是反社會者，只算計對自己最有利的選項。邁克爾則是無知者，忠於公司，信任公司，過去跟這次的提拔都在上層反社會高管的算計中。

So why is promoting over-performing Losers logical? The simple reason is that if you over-perform at the Loser level, it is clear that you are an idiot. You’ve already made a bad bargain, and now you’re delivering more value than you need to, making your bargain even worse. Unless you very quickly demonstrate that you know your own value by successfully negotiating more money and/or power, you are marked out as an exploitable clueless Loser. At one point, Darryl, angling for a raise, learns to his astonishment that the raise he is asking for would make his salary higher than Michael’s. Michael hasn’t negotiated a better deal in 14 years. Darryl — a minimum-effort Loser with strains of Sociopath — doesn’t miss a step. He convinces and coaches Michael into asking for his own raise, so he can get his.

那麽，為什麽晉升業績過剩的失敗者是合乎邏輯的呢？原因很簡單，因為如果你在“失敗者”級別上表現過度，很明顯你就是個白癡。你已經做了一個糟糕的交易，現在你又提供了比你需要的更多的價值，使你的交易變得更加糟糕。除非你能很快通過成功談判獲得更多的金錢和/或權力來證明你了解自己的價值，否則你就會被打上可被利用的無知 Loser 的標籤。有一次，達裏爾想加薪，他驚訝地發現，他要求的加薪將使他的薪水高於邁克爾的薪水。邁克爾已經 14 年沒有談成過更好的條件了。達裏爾–一個有反社會傾向的付出最低限度努力的失敗者–沒有錯過任何一個步驟。他說服並指導邁克爾要求自己加薪，這樣他就能得到自己的加薪。

大意：為什麼要晉升過度努力的失敗者，因為代表它是無知者，容易受騙，也熱衷於自欺欺人。 劇情：倉庫員工 Darryl，平常是摸魚失敗者，但是有反社會的潛力，希望加薪，會馬上行動，勸主管去談成主管自己的加薪，然後他自己才能成功加薪。邁克爾身為無知者主管卻 14 年沒想過要去爭取加薪，因為他習慣於過度付出努力，不能也不敢正確認清自己的價值。

A Loser who can be suckered into bad bargains is set to become one of the Clueless. That’s why they are promoted: they are worth even more as Clueless pawns in the middle than as direct producers at the bottom, where the average, rationally-disengaged Loser will do. At the bottom, the overperformers can merely add a predictable amount of value. In the middle they can be used by the Sociopaths to escape the consequences of high-risk machinations like re-orgs.

一個會被低劣交易吸引的失敗者注定會成為無知者中的一員。這就是他們被提拔的原因：作為中間層的“無知者”棋子，他們的價值甚至高於作為底層直接生產者的價值。在底層，績優者只能增加可預測的價值。在中層，反社會者可以利用他們來逃避重新合併等高風險陰謀的後果。

大意：無知者的無知，更適合用作未來操弄陰謀下的棋子

The Career of the Sociopath 反社會者的職業生涯

The example of the “fast-track the under-performing” part of the principle is Ryan, the intern. He tests himself quickly and rapidly learns and accepts that he is incompetent as a salesman. But he is a born pragmatist with the drive, ambition, daring and lack of principles to make it to the top. So rather than waste time trying to get good at sales, he slips into a wait-watch-grab opportunist mode. But he isn’t checked out; he is engaged, but in an experimental way, probing for his opening. The difference between him and the average checked-out Loser is illustrated in one brilliant scene early in his career. He suggests, during a group stacking effort in the warehouse, that they form a bucket brigade to work more efficiently. The minimum-effort Loser Stanley tells him coldly, “this here is a run-out-the-clock situation.” The line could apply to Stanley’s entire life.

實習生瑞安就是“快速提升表現不佳者”原則的一個例子。他很快就對自己進行了測試，並迅速了解和接受了自己不勝任銷售員的事實。但他是一個天生的實用主義者，有衝勁、有野心、敢於嘗試、不講原則，能夠讓他成功晉升。因此，他沒有浪費時間去努力提高自己的銷售能力，而是進入了一種等待-觀察-抓住機會的模式。但他並沒有心不在焉，而是以一種實驗的方式參與其中，探尋自己的機會。在他職業生涯的早期，有一個精彩的場景說明了他與一般心不在焉的失敗者之間的區別。在倉庫的一次集體堆垛工作中，他建議大家組成一個水桶隊(※排隊長龍傳遞)，以提高工作效率。付出最低限度努力的 Loser 斯坦利冷冷地對他說：“現在的情況是’能消磨掉時間就好’”。這句話可以適用於斯坦利的一生。

大意：反社會者在初期當失敗者的階段，不會浪費時間在一般工作表現良好(假如自身能力不足，不會強求改進)，而是眼光精準的找機會。

Stanley’s response shows both his intelligence and clear-eyed self-awareness of his Loser bargain with the company. He therefore acts according to a mix of self-preservation and minimum-effort coasting instincts. The same is true of everybody else in the Loser layer with the exception of the over-performers: Dwight and Andy (and in his earlier incarnation as a salesperson, Michael).

斯坦利的回答既顯示了他的聰明才智，也表明他清醒地認識到自己是公司的“失敗者”。因此，他的行為既有自我保護意識，也有最低限度的努力的摸魚本能。其他失敗者層的人也是如此，除了表現優異者之外： 德懷特和安迪（以及早期作為銷售員的邁克爾）。

大意：清醒的失敗者，也懂得自保跟摸魚。過度表現的是例外，以後會變成無知者。

The future Sociopath must be an under-performer at the bottom. Like the average Loser, he recognizes that the bargain is a really bad one. Unlike the risk-averse loser though, he does not try to make the best of a bad situation by doing enough to get by. He has no intention of just getting by. He very quickly figures out — through experiments and fast failures — that the Loser game is not worth becoming good at. He then severely under-performs in order to free up energy to concentrate on maneuvering an upward exit. He knows his under-performance is not sustainable, but he has no intention of becoming a lifetime-Loser employee anyway. He takes the calculated risk that he’ll find a way up before he is fired for incompetence.

未來的反社會者一定是最底層的表現不佳者。和一般的失敗者一樣，他也意識到這是一個非常糟糕的交易。但與規避風險的失敗者不同的是，他不會試圖用摸魚過關來減少損失。他無意勉強度日。通過實驗和快速失敗，他很快就發現失敗者遊戲並不值得他去擅長。於是，為了騰出精力專注於向上脫離的操作，他嚴重地表現不佳。他知道自己的表現不佳是不可持續的，但無論如何，他都無意成為終生的 Loser。他經過深思熟慮，認為自己會在因不稱職而被解雇之前找到上升的出路。

大意：有企圖成為反社會者的失敗者，不會滿足於摸魚，也一樣也會故意表現不佳，目的是為了節省精力，到機會出現的時候用功。表現不佳有可能害他被解雇，但是他願意冒險。

Ryan’s character displays this path brilliantly. When Michael’s boss and dominatrix-lover Jan suffers a psychotic meltdown, her boss, the uber-sociopath David Wallace, has no great hopes of a good outcome. Setting up yet another band-aid move, he calls up Michael for an interview to take up Jan’s spot. But when the rest of the office learns of Michael’s impending interview (during Michael’s farcical attempts at using a Survivor style contest to choose his successor, which predictably, only Dwight takes seriously), the true Sociopaths act. Jim and his Sociopath girlfriend Karen instantly call up David and announce their candidacies for the same position. Unknown to them, Ryan, the intern-turned-rookie, has also spotted the opportunity. The outcome is spectacular: Ryan gets the job, Michael loses, Karen is promoted to manager of the Utica branch, and Jim — who still has not yet completely embraced his inner Sociopath — returns to Scranton. We learn later — as the Gervais principle would predict — that David Wallace never seriously considered Michael more than a temporary last resort. Much later, in a deposition during Jan’s lawsuit against the company, he reveals that Michael was never a serious candidate.

瑞安飾演的角色將這一路徑展現得淋漓盡致。當邁克爾的上司兼霸道愛人簡精神崩潰時，她的上司、超級反社會者大衛-華萊士（David Wallace）對好的結果不抱太大希望。他又想出了一個“創可貼”的招數，叫邁克爾去面試，接替簡的位置。但是，當辦公室裏的其他人得知邁克爾即將參加面試時（在邁克爾試圖用“幸存者”式的比賽來選擇繼任者的鬧劇中，可想而知，只有德懷特認真對待了），真正的反社會者行動了。吉姆和他的反社會女友凱倫立刻打電話給大衛，宣布他們將競選同一職位。他們不知道的是，由實習生變成菜鳥的瑞安也發現了這個機會。結果可想而知：瑞安得到了這份工作，邁克爾落選，凱倫被提拔為尤蒂卡分行的經理，而吉姆–他還沒有完全接受自己內心反社會的一面–回到了斯克蘭頓。我們後來才知道–正如熱爾韋原理所預言的那樣–大衛-華萊士從未認真考慮過邁克爾，而只是把他當作臨時的最後手段。很久以後，在簡對公司提起訴訟期間的證詞中，他透露邁克爾從來都不是一個認真的候選人。

大意：劇情裡反社會者抓住機會晉升的例子。無知者不是長久考慮的人選，但可以充當臨時的棋子。

The Career of the Loser 失敗者的職業生涯

The career of the Loser is the easiest to understand. Having made a bad bargain, and not marked for either Clueless or Sociopath trajectories, he or she must make the best of a bad situation. The most rational thing to do is slack off and do the minimum necessary. Doing more would be a Clueless thing to do. Doing less would take the high-energy machinations of the Sociopath, since it sets up self-imposed up-or-out time pressure. So the Loser — really not a loser at all if you think about it — pays his dues, does not ask for much, and finds meaning in his life elsewhere. For Stanley it is crossword puzzles. For Angela it is a colorless Martha-Stewartish religious life. For Kevin, it is his rock band. For Kelly, it is mindless airhead pop-culture distractions. Pam has her painting ambitions. Meredith is an alcoholic slut. Oscar, the ironic-token gay character, has his intellectual posturing. Creed, a walking freak-show, marches to the beat of his own obscure different drum (he is the most rationally checked-out of all the losers).

失敗者的職業生涯最容易理解。他（她）做了一筆不划算的交易，而且沒有被標記為“無知”或“反社會”的軌跡，他（她）必須在糟糕的情況下做出最好的選擇。 最理性的做法就是偷懶，做最起碼的事。做得更多是無知的表現。而少做則需要反社會者的高能機智，因為這會給自己帶來時間上的壓力。因此，“失敗者”–如果你仔細想想，其實一點也不失敗–只做該做的事，不求太多，在別處尋找生活的意義。對斯坦利來說，這就是填字遊戲。對安吉拉來說，是毫無色彩的瑪莎-斯圖爾特式(※家庭主婦)的宗教生活。對凱文來說，是他的搖滾樂隊。對凱利來說，是無意識的頭腦空空流行文化。帕姆有她的繪畫野心。梅雷迪思是個酗酒的蕩婦。奧斯卡是一個具有諷刺意味的同性戀角色，他擺出一副知識分子的姿態。克裏德是個行走的怪胎，按著自己晦澀難懂的不同鼓點前進（他是所有失敗者中最理性地心不在焉的一個）。

大意：失敗者對工作心不在焉，因為知道太努力對自己更不划算，轉而追求個人的興趣嗜好。

If you leave out the clear marked-for-Clueless characters, Dwight and Andy, you are left with the two most interesting characters in the show: the will-he-won’t-he Sociopath-in-the-making, Jim, and the strange Toby. Toby is a curious case — intellectually a Sociopath, but without the energy or ambition to be an active sociopath. More about these two later.

如果撇開德懷特（Dwight）和安迪（Andy）這兩個明顯的“標記將成為無知者”的角色，那麽劇中就剩下兩個最有趣的角色了：一個是“讓人好奇他會不會”的“成形中的反社會者”吉姆（Jim），另一個是奇怪的托比（Toby）。托比是一個奇怪的案例–在智力上是一個反社會者，但卻沒有精力或野心成為一個積極的反社會者。稍後再詳細介紹這兩個人。

大意：劇情中有兩個失敗者人物，具有反社會者的認知，但沒有積極行為。

The Emergence of the MacLeod Hierarchy 麥克勞德等級制度的出現

Dastardly as all this sounds, it is actually pretty efficient, given the inevitability of the MacLeod hierarchy and life cycle. The Sociopaths know that the only way to make an organization capable of survival is to buffer the intense chemistry between the producer-Losers and the leader-Sociopaths with enough Clueless padding in the middle to mitigate the risks of business. Without it, the company would explode like a nuclear bomb, rather than generate power steadily like a reactor. On the other hand, the business wouldn’t survive very long without enough people actually thinking in cold, calculating ways. The average-performing , mostly-disengaged Losers can create diminishing-margins profitability, but not sustainable performance or growth. You need a steady supply of Sociopaths for that, and you cannot waste time moving them slowly up the ranks, especially since the standard promotion/development path is primarily designed to maneuver the Clueless into position wherever they are needed. The Sociopaths must be freed up as much as possible to actually run the business, with or without official titles.

雖然這一切聽起來很卑鄙，但考慮到麥克勞德等級制度和生命周期的必然性，這實際上是非常有效率的。反社會者知道，使組織能夠生存下去的唯一方法，就是緩衝生產者–失敗者和領導者–反社會者之間的激烈化學反應，在中間加入足夠多的無知者，以降低經營風險。如果沒有這些，公司就會像核彈一樣爆炸，而不是像反應堆一樣穩定發電。另一方面，如果沒有足夠多的人以冷酷、精打細算的方式思考問題，企業也不會存活太久。表現平平、大多不思進取的失敗者可以創造遞減的利潤率，但無法創造可持續的業績或增長。為此，你需要源源不斷地提供反社會者，而且你不能浪費時間讓他們慢慢晉升，尤其是標準的晉升/發展途徑主要是為了把無知者安排到需要他們的地方。反社會者必須盡可能騰出時間來實際經營業務，無論有沒有正式頭銜。

大意：無知者的用途是居中緩衝，而反社會者才能提供衝勁增長。要補充反社會者的數量，才能維繫組織生存。所以高層的反社會者為了要補充，會快速拔擢失敗者中的好苗子，假如讓他慢慢走標準晉升路線就會來不及，而且升上無知者只能發揮緩衝功能，而不是高層期望看到的貢獻。

So Ryan floats directly to the top, where he does what is expected of him — lead a bold strategic gamble by building an online sales channel operation. As with any big strategic move, the operation has its risks, and fails. And here we find that Ryan is still not quite experienced enough as a sociopath. He foolishly goes the Enron route, attempting to cook the books to avoid failure, and is found out and arrested. A true master Sociopath like David Wallace would instead have spotted the impending failure, promoted a Michael to take over (who would obviously be so gratified at being given a new white-elephant title that he would not have seen disaster looming), and have him take the blame for the inevitable failure. Completely legal and efficient.

因此，瑞安直接升到了高層，在那裏，他做著人們對他的期望–領導一場大膽的戰略賭博，建立一個在線銷售渠道。與任何大的戰略舉措一樣，這項行動也有風險，也會失敗。在這裏，我們發現瑞安作為反社會者的經驗還不夠豐富。他愚蠢地走上了安然公司(※詐欺)的老路，試圖做假帳來避免失敗，結果被發現並逮捕。而像大衛-華萊士這樣真正的反社會大師則會發現即將發生的失敗，提拔邁克爾接管公司（他顯然會因為被授予新的白象頭銜而欣喜若狂，以至於看不到災難正在逼近），並讓他為不可避免的失敗承擔責任。完全合法且高效。

大意：無知者可以被反社會者當成陰謀出賣的棄子。反社會者會做開創的舉措，劇情中瑞安的戰略賭博事實上是高層期望的，雖然失敗了而且忘了安排後路。

The Organization as Psychic Prison 作為心靈監獄的組織

Which brings us to the other major management book that is consistent with the Gervais Principle, *Images of Organization, Gareth Morgan’s magisterial study of the metaphors through which we understand organizations. Of the eight systemic metaphors in the book, the one that is most relevant here is the metaphor of an organization as a psychic prison. The image is derived from Plato’s allegory of the cave, which I won’t get into here. Suffice it to say that it divides people into those who get how the world really works (the Sociopaths and the self-aware slacker Losers) and those who don’t (the over-performer Losers and the Clueless in the middle).

這就引出了另一本與熱爾韋原理相一致的重要管理書籍《組織的形象》，加雷思-摩根（Gareth Morgan）對我們理解組織的隱喻進行了深入研究。在書中的八個系統隱喻中，與此最為相關的是將組織比喻為心靈監獄的隱喻。這個隱喻源自柏拉圖的洞穴寓言，在此我就不多說了。我只想說，它把人分成了兩類，一類是了解世界真正運作方式的人（反社會者和自覺偷懶的失敗者），另一類是不了解的人（表現出色的失敗者和處於中間的無知者）。

大意：將組織比喻為心靈監獄的隱喻，過度表現的失敗者跟無知者，是受困其中的不清醒的人。

This is where Gervais has broken new ground, primarily because as an artist, he is interested in the subjective experience of being Clueless (most sitcoms are about Losers). For your everyday Sociopath, it is sufficient to label someone clueless and manipulate them. What Gervais managed to create is a very compelling portrait of the Clueless, a work of art with real business value.

這正是熱維斯開辟新天地的地方，主要是因為作為一名藝術家，他對“無知者”的主觀體驗很感興趣（大多數情景喜劇都是關於“失敗者”的）。對於日常的反社會者來說，給人貼上“無知者”的標籤並操縱他們就足夠了。熱爾韋成功地塑造了一個非常引人注目的“無知者”形象，這是一部具有真正商業價值的藝術作品。

大意：《辦公室》的作者提供無知者做主角的主觀體驗，是喜劇中少見的。

Here is the ultimate explanation of Michael Scott’s (and David Brent’s) careers: they are put into a position of having to explain their own apparent, unexpected and unexamined success. It is easy to explain failure. Random success is harder. Remember, they are promoted primarily as passive pawns to either allow the Sociopaths to escape the risks of their actions, or to make way for the Sociopaths to move up faster. They are presented with an interesting bit of cognitive dissonance: being nominally given greater power, but in reality being safely shunted away from the pathways of power. They must choose to either construct false narratives or decline apparent opportunities.

這就是邁克爾-斯科特（和大衛-布倫特）職業生涯的最終解釋：他們被置於不得不解釋自己明顯的、出乎意料的和未經審視的成功的境地。解釋失敗很容易。偶然的成功則很難解釋。請記住，他們主要是作為被動的棋子被提拔的，要麽讓反社會者逃避他們行為的風險，要麽為反社會者更快地晉升讓路。他們面臨著一種有趣的認知失調：名義上被賦予了更大的權力，但實際上卻被安全地避開了權力之路。他們必須做出選擇，要麽編造虛假的故事，要麽拒絕明顯的機會。

大意：無知者名義上有權利，事實卻沒有。為了處理認知失調，需要自欺。拒絕的機會指的是無知者拒絕成為清醒的人，脫離對組織的妄想。

The Clueless resolve this dissonance by choosing to believe in the reality of the organization. Not everybody is capable of this level of suspension of disbelief. Both Ricky Gervais (David Brent) and Steve Carrel (Michael Scott) play the brilliantly drawn characters perfectly. The most visible sign of their capacity for self-delusion is their complete inability to generate an original thought. They quote movie lines, lyrics and perform terrible impersonations (at one point Michael goes, “You talking to me?” a line he attributes, in a masterful display of confusion, to “Al Pacino, Raging Bull“). For much of what he needs to say, he gropes for empty business phrases, deploying them with staggering incompetence. When Michael talks, he is attempting, like a child, to copy the flawless Powertalk spoken by sociopaths like Jan and David Wallace. He is oblivious to the fact that the Sociopaths use Powertalk as a coded language with which to simultaneously sustain the (necessary) delusions of the Clueless and communicate with each other.

無知者通過選擇相信組織的現實來解決這種不協調。並不是每個人都能做到這種程度。瑞奇-熱爾韋（Ricky Gervais，飾演大衛-布倫特）和史蒂夫-卡雷爾（Steve Carrel，飾演邁克爾-斯科特）都完美地演繹了這一出色的角色。他們自欺欺人的能力最明顯的表現就是完全無法產生原創性的想法。他們引用電影臺詞和歌詞，模仿得惟妙惟肖（有一次邁克爾說：“你在跟我說話嗎？”他把這句臺詞歸結為“阿爾-帕西諾，《憤怒的公牛》”）。對於他需要表達的大部分內容，他都是在摸索空洞的商業用語，並且在使用這些用語時表現出令人吃驚的無能。當邁克爾說話時，他就像個孩子一樣，試圖模仿簡和大衛-華萊士這樣的反社會者所說的完美無瑕的“權力話語”。他渾然不知，反社會者使用“Powertalk”作為一種編碼語言，既能維持“無知者”的（必要）妄想，又能相互交流。

大意：無知者對組織很買帳，有助於他自欺。自欺也表現在他沒有原創想法，喜歡模仿電影台詞跟反社會高層的權力話語，而並不清楚內涵。

It is not just the Sociopaths who conspire to sustain Michael’s delusions. So do the checked-out Losers, sometimes out of kindness, and sometimes out of self-interest. In one particularly perfect summing up, Oscar describes the impending “Dundies” award ceremony (a veritable monument to the consensual enablement of Michael’s delusions) as “The Dundies are kind of like a kid’s birthday party. And you go, and there’s really nothing for you to do there, but the kid’s having a really good time, so you… You’re kind of there. That’s… That’s kind of what it’s like.”

不僅僅是反社會者合謀維持邁克爾的妄想。心不在焉的失敗者也是如此，他們有時出於善意，有時出於私利。在一次特別完美的總結中(※偽紀錄片採訪)，奧斯卡把即將舉行的“鄧迪獎”頒獎典禮（名副其實的紀念碑，標誌著邁克爾的妄想得到了大家一致認可的助長）描述成：“鄧迪獎有點像孩子的生日派對。你去了，在那裏你什麽也做不了，但孩子們玩得很開心，所以你……你就在那裏。這就是那種感覺”。

大意：失敗者也會助長無知者的妄想，看破不說破。

But Michael isn’t entirely a puppet. Buried under layers of denial is a clear understanding of his own, hopeless, powerless life, which makes him marginally more clued-in than say, Dwight. His response is frenetic and desperate manipulation of the drama of false validation that has been set up for his benefit. Some of this is with the knowing consent of his enablers. Like experienced improv-comics, within limits, the rest of the office follows the rule of agreement in the Theater of Michael (in a brilliant piece of meta-commentary, in one episode we get to see Michael at his own impossibly bad worst in his real improv class, where he ruins every single sketch).

但邁克爾並不完全是個傀儡。在層層否認之下，他對自己無望無力的生活有清晰的認識，這讓他比德懷特稍微清醒一些。他的反應是狂熱而絕望地操縱為他的利益而設置的虛假驗證戲劇。這其中有些是在他的助長者知情同意的情況下進行的。就像經驗豐富的即興喜劇演員一樣，在邁克爾的劇場裏，辦公室裏的其他人都會在一定範圍內遵守約定俗成的規則（在一集中，我們可以看到邁克爾在他真正的即興喜劇課上的糟糕表現，他毀掉了每一個小品）。

大意：無知者隱約察覺到自己處境，於是更為投入的自欺演出，以讓自己好過一點。

But Michael’s grand narrative requires constant, exhausting work to keep up. He must amplify and rope in even the most minor piece of validation into the service of his script. When, in a moment of weakness, Jim shares a genuine confidence with him, Michael is so thrilled that he turns the moment into a deep imaginary friendship, practically becoming a stalker, even mimicking Jim’s hairstyle. At the other end, he over-represses even the slightest potential dent to his self-image. His is a thin-skinnedness gone crazy. Reality is sealed away with psychotic urgency, but to do so, he must first scout it out with equal urgency. And so, when Jim (in the first true Sociopath move of his career) engineers a private meeting with the visiting David Wallace to carve out a promotion, Michael tries to crash the meeting. When politely turned away, he instantly switches scripts and pretends he is too busy and that he is the one who can’t attend. And then he sneaks into the meeting room anyway, first with various excuses, and finally by hiding in a Trojan-Horse cheese cart.

但是，邁克爾的宏大敘事需要持續不斷的努力才能跟上。即使是最微不足道的片段，他也必須將其放大，為劇本服務。當吉姆在脆弱的時候與他分享了一份真誠的信任時，邁克爾非常激動，他把這一刻變成了深厚的假想友誼，幾乎成了一個跟蹤狂，甚至模仿吉姆的髮型。 另一方面，即使是最輕微的潛在自我形象損害，他也會過度緊張。他的臉皮薄到了瘋狂的地步。他急切地想把現實封印起來，但要做到這一點，他必須先以同樣的急切心情把現實探查清楚。因此，當吉姆（在他的職業生涯中第一次真正意義上的反社會舉動）策劃與來訪的大衛-華萊士（David Wallace）私下會面以謀求晉升時，邁克爾試圖破壞這次會面。當被禮貌地拒絕後，他立刻切換劇本，假裝自己太忙，是他不能參加會議。然後，他還是偷偷溜進了會議室，先是找各種借口，最後躲進了一輛特洛伊木馬奶酪車裏。

大意：在無知者自欺編造的劇本中，自我美好的形象與脆弱易受傷的臉皮，來回拉扯。

This sort of ability to work hard to sustain the theater of his own delusions, half-aware that he is doing so, is what makes Michael a genuine candidate for promotion to the ranks of the Clueless. Dwight is interesting precisely because he lacks Michael’s capacity for this pathological meta-cognition, and the ability to offer semi-believable scripts that others can at least help bolster. Dwight is not talented enough at Cluelessness to ever be promoted.

這種努力維持自己妄想的能力，讓邁克爾成為真正的“無知者”候選人。德懷特之所以有趣，正是因為他缺乏邁克爾這種病態的元認知能力，以及提供別人至少可以幫助支持的半可信劇本的能力。德懷特在“無知者”方面沒有足夠的天賦，因此無法晉升。

大意：德懷特的妄想自欺功力還不足以讓他升上無知者主管的位子。他有時會清醒認知到自己不足之處，身邊的人很解嗨，不能跳進來陪他演戲。

Is There More? 還有嗎？

You bet. We haven’t even scratched the surface. Dwight, Jim, and Toby each deserve an entire essay. Michael and Ryan probably deserve one each as well, in addition to my quick sketches here. And there are other principles, lemmas and sundry theoretical constructs. But I’ll hold off. Maybe there aren’t as many Office watchers among this blog’s readers as I imagine. You guys tell me if you want more.

當然還有。我們甚至還沒有觸及表面。德懷特、吉姆和托比每個人都值得寫一整篇文章。邁克爾和瑞安可能也值得各寫一篇，此外還有我在這裏的速寫。還有其他一些原則、定理和雜七雜八的理論構架。但我還是不說了。也許本博客的讀者中沒有我想象的那麽多《辦公室》觀察者。如果你們想了解更多，請告訴我。

I’ll conclude with one thought: Gervais deserves Nobel prizes in both literature and economics.

最後，我有一個想法： 熱爾韋應該獲得諾貝爾文學獎和諾貝爾經濟學獎。
