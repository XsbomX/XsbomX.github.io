The Gervais Principle P5

熱爾韋原理 5

The Gervais Principle V: Heads I Win, Tails You Lose

October 14, 2011 By Venkatesh Rao

原文網址:
https://www.ribbonfarm.com/2011/10/14/the-gervais-principle-v-heads-i-win-tails-you-lose/

At the heart of all tragedy, the Greeks saw a phenomenon they called hamartia: a fatal error born of unavoidable ignorance. Combined with a fundamental moral flaw, hamartia inevitably led on to destruction. For the Greeks, humans were cursed not just with mortality of the flesh, but also hamartia-driven mortality of the spirit. Hamartia was the Gods being Divine Jerks, randomly toying with human lives for their own pleasure, through cat-and-mouse games the latter could not hope to win.

希臘人認為，所有悲劇的核心都是一種被稱為“哈馬提亞”（hamartia）的現象：一種因無法避免的無知而產生的致命錯誤。與基本的道德缺陷相結合，hamartia 不可避免地導致毀滅。在希臘人看來，人類不僅受到肉體死亡的詛咒，而且還受到哈馬提亞驅動的精神死亡的詛咒。哈馬提亞是眾神乃神聖的混蛋，他們為了自己的快樂，通過貓捉老鼠的遊戲，隨意玩弄人類的生命，而後者根本無法取勝。

For the Greeks, any divine purpose, even subtly malicious randomness, in the ordering of the universe, was preferable to purposelessness. At least the gods cared enough to be cruel.

對希臘人來說，宇宙秩序中的任何神聖目的，哪怕是微妙的惡意隨機性，都比沒有目的要好。至少諸神關心到足夠殘酷。

The Gervais Principle P4

熱爾韋原理 4

The Gervais Principle IV: Wonderful Human Beings

October 14, 2010 By Venkatesh Rao

原文網址:
https://www.ribbonfarm.com/2010/10/14/the-gervais-principle-iv-wonderful-human-beings/

Each of them – and they constitute 80% of humanity – is born the most beautiful baby in the world. Each is an above-average child; in fact the entire 80% is in the top 20% of human beings (it’s crowded up there). Each grows up knowing that he or she is deeply special in some way, and destined for a unique life that he or she is “meant” to live.

他們中的每一個–他們佔人類的 80%–都是世界上最漂亮的嬰兒。每個人都是高於平均水平的孩子；事實上，整個 80% 的人都在人類的前 20% 之列（那裏很擁擠）。每個人在成長過程中都知道，自己在某些方面非常特別，註定要過一種自己“命中註定”的獨特生活。

In their troubled twenties, each seeks the one true love that they know is out there, waiting for them, and their real calling in life. Each time they fail at life or love, their friends console them: “You are a smart, funny, beautiful and incredibly talented person, and the love of your life and your true calling are out there somewhere. I just know that.” The friends are right of course: each marries the most beautiful man/woman in the world, discovers his/her calling, and becomes the proud parent of the most beautiful baby in the world. Eventually, each of them retires, earns a gold watch, and somebody makes a speech declaring him or her to be a Wonderful Human Being.

二十多歲時，每個人都在尋找真愛，他們知道真愛就在那裏等著他們，也是他們生命中真正的使命。每當他們的生活或愛情失敗時，朋友們都會安慰他們：“你是一個聰明、風趣、美麗、才華橫溢的人，你的真愛和真正的使命就在某處。我就是知道這一點。”朋友們當然是對的：每個人都嫁給了世界上最美麗的男人/女人，發現了自己的使命，成為世界上最美麗的嬰兒的驕傲的父母。最後，他們每個人都退休了，賺到了一塊金錶，有人發表演講，宣布他或她是一個了不起的人。

The Gervais Principle III: The Curse of Development

April 14, 2010 By Venkatesh Rao

原文網址:
https://www.ribbonfarm.com/2010/04/14/the-gervais-principle-iii-the-curse-of-development/

In the first two parts of this series, we talked about the archetypes that inhabit organizations (Sociopaths, Losers, Clueless), what they do (the Gervais Principle) and how (the four languages). In this part, we’ll use a somewhat unorthodox take on the idea of arrested development to explain why the three groups behave as they do, and use that to predict the outcomes of individual interpersonal interactions.

在本系列的前兩部分中，我們談到了組織中的原型（反社會者、失敗者、無知者）、他們的行為（熱爾韋原理）和方式（四種語言）。在這一部分中，我們將用一種有點非正統的“發展停滯”觀點來解釋為什麼這三個群體是這樣的行為方式，並以此來預測個人人際交往的結果。

For those who came in late: read Part I and Part II first, to avoid serious misunderstandings.

遲到的讀者請先閱讀第一部分和第二部分，以免產生嚴重誤解。

We began this analysis of corporate life by exploring a theoretical construct (the Gervais Principle) through the character arcs of Michael and Ryan in The Office. The construct and examples provide a broad-strokes treatment of the why of the power dynamics among Sociopaths, the Clueless and Losers.

我們通過《辦公室》中邁克爾和瑞安的人物弧線，探索了一種理論構架（熱爾韋原理），從而開始了對企業生活的分析。這一理論構架和實例概括性地闡述了反社會者、無知者和失敗者之間權力動態的原因。

This helps us understand how the world works, but not how to work it. So let me introduce you to the main skill required here, mastery over the four major languages spoken in organizations, among Sociopaths, Losers and the Clueless. I’ll call the four languages Posturetalk, Powertalk, Babytalk and Gametalk. Here’s a picture of who speaks what to whom. Let’s use it to figure out how to make friends and influence people, Office style.

這理論有助於我們理解這個世界是如何運作的，但卻還不知道如何去運用。因此，讓我向你介紹這裏所需的主要技能，即掌握反社會者、失敗者和無知者在組織中所使用的四種主要語言。我把這四種語言稱為 “姿態語”（Posturetalk）、”權力語”（Powertalk）、”寶寶語”（Babytalk）和 “遊戲語”（Gametalk）。下面是誰對誰說什麽的圖片。讓我們用它來了解如何以辦公室風格交友和影響他人。

The Gervais Principle P1

熱爾韋原理 1

The Gervais Principle, Or The Office According to “The Office”

October 7, 2009 By Venkatesh Rao

原文網址:
https://www.ribbonfarm.com/2009/10/07/the-gervais-principle-or-the-office-according-to-the-office/

My neighbor introduced me to The Office back in 2005. Since then, I’ve watched every episode of both the British and American versions. I’ve watched the show obsessively because I’ve been unable to figure out what makes it so devastatingly effective, and elevates it so far above the likes of Dilbert and Office Space.

2005 年，我的鄰居向我介紹了《辦公室風雲》。從那時起，我看完了英國版和美國版的每一集。我之所以癡迷於這部劇，是因為我一直弄不明白是什麽讓它具有如此巨大的殺傷力，讓它遠遠超越了《呆伯特》和《辦公室空間》。

Until now, that is. Now, after four years, I’ve finally figured the show out. The Office is not a random series of cynical gags aimed at momentarily alleviating the existential despair of low-level grunts. It is a fully realized theory of management that falsifies 83.8% of the business section of the bookstore. The theory begins with Hugh MacLeod’s well-known cartoon, Company Hierarchy (below), and its cornerstone is something I will call The Gervais Principle, which supersedes both the Peter Principle and its successor, The Dilbert Principle. Outside of the comic aisle, the only major and significant works consistent with the Gervais Principle are The Organization Man and Images of Organization.

直到現在，就是這樣。四年後的今天，我終於弄明白了這部劇。《辦公室》並不是一連串冷嘲熱諷的插科打諢，其目的也不只是為了暫時緩解基層員工的絕望情緒。它是一個完全實現了的管理理論，其正確性贏過書店中商業區 83.8% 的內容。 該理論始於休-麥克勞德（Hugh MacLeod）的著名漫畫《公司等級制度》（下圖），其基石我稱之為“熱爾韋原理”（The Gervais Principle），它取代了“彼得原理”及其後繼者“呆伯特法則”。在漫畫領域之外，唯一符合熱爾韋原則的重要作品是《組織人》和《組織形象》。
